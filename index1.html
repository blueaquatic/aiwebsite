"They found us. The ones who walk between the trees. We can hear them breathing through the walls. Sarah says we should leave, but the doors won't open anymore. The watches have all stopped. Time doesn't work the same way here. If you're reading this, DO NOT STAY AFTER DARK."

File No. 2008-MA-156

THE SMILING FAMILY

In 2008, the Whitmore family of six was found in their Massachusetts home, all seated at the dinner table. They had been deceased for approximately three weeks. What disturbed investigators wasn't the tragedy itself, but the fact that all six family members, including the children, were found smiling. The coroner reported this was not rigor mortis - their facial muscles had been deliberately positioned.
Detective Morrison's Notes:
"I've worked homicide for 20 years. I've never seen anything like this. The scene was arranged like a normal family dinner - plates set, food still on the table (long spoiled). But every single one of them was smiling. Not a natural smile. Too wide. The children's faces especially... it haunts me. There was no sign of struggle. No forced entry. The doors were locked from the inside. Toxicology came back clean. We found a camera set on a timer. It took photos every hour for three weeks. In every single photo, they're sitting there. Smiling. We can't explain how. They should have shown signs of decomposition, of movement, something. But in every photo - just sitting there, smiling at the camera."
